About Me

My name is Hersh Thakkar, and I am a computer science student at Purdue University with a passion for creating innovative software solutions. I specialize in frontend design, database management, and mobile application development, blending technical skills with creative problem-solving to build impactful digital experiences. This portfolio showcases my work on projects for CGT 17208, ranging from research on the Starship robotic systems to a fitness mobile application, reflecting my commitment to user-centered design and technology-driven innovation.

Exercise 1 - User Experience in Software Development

This exercise focused on understanding the critical role of User Experience (UX) in software development. Key takeaways included:

  • Core Focus: Designing intuitive, accessible, and engaging software products to create positive user outcomes.
  • Key Skills: Frontend technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React), prototyping tools (Figma, Sketch), and usability testing frameworks.
  • Technical Skills: User research, data analysis, and iterative design methodologies.
  • Soft Skills: Empathy, collaboration, and effective communication for building user-centered designs.
  • Why UX Matters: Enhances user satisfaction, reduces learning curves, and drives business success by aligning technology with real-world needs.

Project 1 - Starship Research

This project explored the challenges and opportunities in campus delivery systems using Starship robots. My contributions included:

  • User Research: Conducted interviews and documented user frustrations with delivery speed, app reliability, and order accuracy.
  • Design Proposals: Developed innovative solutions like 'Tour Guide Starships' for campus navigation and 'Guardian Starships' with facial recognition for safety.
  • Technical Insights: Highlighted the need for improved routing algorithms and real-time communication to reduce delivery times.
  • Impact: Presented a vision for smarter, safer, and more efficient robot delivery networks, potentially transforming campus logistics.

Project 2 - Embodied Shopping

This project aimed to redefine the in-store shopping experience using augmented reality (AR). My work included:

  • Concept Ideation: Developed the Smart Cart Companion, an early concept for personalized, tech-enhanced shopping carts.
  • Journey Mapping: Created user journey maps to identify pain points and optimize customer experience in physical retail environments.
  • Design Prototyping: Contributed to the Figma design of the virtual shopping assistant interface, including real-time stock tracking and product recommendations.
  • Reflections: Gained insights into the challenges of blending digital and physical shopping experiences.

Project 3 - Fake Out

This project tackled the issue of 'exercise fake outs' in fitness tracking. My contributions included:

  • Problem Identification: Identified key issues with step-based metrics and their impact on user motivation.
  • Community Features: Designed peer support and accountability systems to foster real engagement.
  • Authenticity Verification: Developed AI-powered systems to detect and discourage cheating in fitness metrics.
  • Long-Term Strategy: Focused on sustainable fitness habits, promoting real-world health outcomes.
